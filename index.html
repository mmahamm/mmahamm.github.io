About Us

Mission

Refugee Integration, Stability, and Education (R.I.S.E.).

Purpose

R.I.S.E. empowers refugee youth through tutoring and community engagement activities that foster their ongoing professional, academic, and social development--features key to enabling sustainable upward mobility. Over the past 2.5 years, R.I.S.E. has developed a strong relationship with refugees residing in Phoenix, Arizona by providing weekly tutoring in various academic disciplines. The goal of the program is to expand into a multifaceted institution that offers vehicles for advancement to refugee students by not only improving their academic performance, but also by facilitating their professional development and social integration.

History

This organization was first initiated in the Summer of 2013 by the AMWA Education Committee Chair, Lubna Ahmed, who recruited tutors to educate refugee kids. Much of the establishment of the program is credited to Maryam Waris (one of the first tutors). Maryam helped by recruiting the majority of tutors you see before you and rebranding this Organization into R.I.S.E. in the Winter of 2015. We are currently being funded by American Muslim Women’s Association (AMWA), and have been a student organization on the ASU Tempe campus for 2 years now.
